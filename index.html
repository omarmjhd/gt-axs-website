About Us

Alpha Chi Sigma (AXΣ) is a professional chemistry fraternity - an organization whose goal is to promote the interests of the chemical profession in all its forms. Membership is open to collegiate and professional men and women who possess a strong interest in chemistry, and is for life. Being a professional fraternity, AXΣ aims to bridge the divide between the collegiate and professional, allowing members to experience firsthand the kind of work they will encounter in their careers as chemists. This is accomplished through Professional and Service Activities, such as our joint project with the Boy Scouts of America. All chemistry related majors are welcome to join. This includes Chemistry, Biology, Biochemistry, CHBE, BME, EAS, MSE, and all majors on the Pre-Med track.

Here is a copy of our Bylaws. Please feel free to take a look.

To bind its members with a tie of true and lasting friendship.

To strive for the advancement of chemistry both as a science and as a profession.

To aid its members by every honorable means in the attainment of their ambitions as chemists throughout their mortal lives.

53rd Biennial Conclave

Conclave is a meeting held in even numbered years where Brothers of the Fraternity gather to conduct national Fraternity business, to exchange ideas about chapter operations and to interact with each other to further the Objects of the Fraternity. The 53rd Biennial Conclave will be hosted by our chapter from July 25, 2016 to July 30, 2016.

Check out our Facebook page or the National Website for more information and how to register.

Boy Scouts Science Outreach

Every Fall and Spring semester, Alpha Chi Sigma hosts a group of young scouts from local Boy Scouts of America troops and helps them earn their Chemistry merit badge. Small groups of brothers give presentations to the scouts about basic concepts in chemistry, careers in chemistry, and chemistry's impact on the environment. The entire event concludes with a demonstration of several exciting, eye-catching demonstrations of powerful chemical reactions. This is always a favorite event amongst brothers, as it gives us the chance to bring young minds into the wonderful world of chemistry and make a meaningful impact in their education.

Our chapter performs demonstrations both here at Georgia Tech and at various elementary schools around the Atlanta area. Our most popular demos are typically making tie-dye t-shirts and liquid nitrogen ice cream; these are fun not only for spectators but also for the brothers performing them!
